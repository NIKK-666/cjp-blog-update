Origin Story

THE SLUR THAT STARTED A REVOLUTION

On May 15, 2026, inside a Supreme Court chamber, Chief Justice of India Surya Kant made remarks that would ignite the biggest youth protest movement India had seen in years. During an open court hearing, he compared unemployed young Indians to "cockroaches" and "parasites of society."

"There are youngsters like cockroaches, they don't get any employment, they don't have any place in profession. Some of them become media, some of them become social media, RTI activists and other activists, and they start attacking everyone."

— Chief Justice Surya Kant, Supreme Court of India, May 15, 2026

Kant later issued a clarification, insisting his words were misquoted and that he was specifically referring to people who entered the legal profession using fake degrees — not India's youth at large. But the damage was done. The word "cockroach" had already landed.

Within hours, Abhijeet Dipke — a 30-year-old political communications strategist and Boston University student who had once worked with the Aam Aadmi Party — posted a question on social media: "What if all cockroaches come together?"

That single line would become the founding spark of the Cockroach Janta Party.

The Manifesto

RTI Transparency

Absolute transparency and full answerability under the Right to Information Act. Every public office must be accountable to citizens, not shielded by bureaucratic walls.

🚫

Ban Electoral Bonds

A strict ban on anonymous political donations and electoral bonds. Voters deserve to know who funds their leaders and what hidden agendas come with that money.

💼

Creation of a Right to Employment law that guarantees either a job or an unemployment allowance for every Indian citizen between the ages of 21 and 60.

Full accountability for the NEET paper leak scandal of 2024. Demand for the resignation of Union Education Minister Dharmendra Pradhan, with structural reforms to prevent exam fraud.

End to judicial corruption and post-retirement rewards for judges who serve political interests. The Supreme Court must be independent, impartial, and answerable to the people.

Analysis

WHY THIS MATTERS

The CJP isn't just a meme. It's a mirror held up to a generation that inherited record unemployment, repeated exam scandals, and institutions they no longer trust.

The Youth Crisis

India's youth unemployment hovers in double digits by conservative estimates. The 2024 NEET paper leak destroyed the aspirations of millions of students who had spent years and family savings preparing for one exam. The CJP gave this frustration a face — and a name.

Digital Dissent

The CJP's Instagram account crossed 19 million followers in under a week — nearly double the ruling BJP. This is the first time in Indian history that a satirical online "party" outpaced real political parties in social media reach, signalling a seismic shift in how India's youth engage with politics.

The Cockroach Metaphor

By embracing the cockroach, the movement turned a slur into a symbol of resilience. It describes itself as "Secular, Socialist, Democratic, and Lazy" — a tongue-in-cheek inversion of every political promise ever broken. The movement has since spread to West Bengal, Bihar, UP, Haryana, Jammu & Kashmir, and Himachal Pradesh.
